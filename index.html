The Real Problem: A Credit Market Failure

In Brazil, the education gap is not primarily a problem of demand or institutional capacity — it’s a systemic underwriting failure. Traditional credit systems evaluate history (credit score), not future earning potential, excluding millions of capable students.

The Credit Trap

Millions are “negativados” or lack credit history, so banks reject them regardless of academic merit or future income potential.

The Dropout Crisis

Private institutions absorb most demand, yet dropout rates remain high due to financial instability — financing fails at entry and throughout the journey.

Misaligned Incentives

Banks want safe portfolios, universities want stable enrollment, and students want dignity — but the system lacks a shared risk framework.
